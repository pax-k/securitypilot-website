What does penetration testing mean? How does it work?

Penetration testing, or pentesting, is a simulated cyber attack against your computer system, website, or network to check for exploitable vulnerabilities. In the context of web security, it involves testing your web applications for flaws or weaknesses. Our ethical hackers use the same techniques as attackers to identify security holes, but instead of exploiting these vulnerabilities, they report them back to you. This process provides insights into how an attacker could breach your system, allowing you to make informed decisions about how to strengthen your defenses.

What is threat intelligence?

Threat intelligence is the collection and analysis of information about existing and emerging threats to your cybersecurity. It's a proactive defense mechanism that enables businesses to understand the risks posed by hackers, malware, and other cyber threats. By analyzing data from various sources, SecurityPilot provides actionable insights, helping you to anticipate, prevent, and mitigate potential cyber attacks effectively.
